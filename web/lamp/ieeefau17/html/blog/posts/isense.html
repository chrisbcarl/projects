Request for Computer Science and Computer Engineering Students for I-SENSE

by R Raldiris

Posted on March 28, 2016

Here is an another great opportunity for Undergraduate Computer Science/Computer Engineering Students.

I-SENSE the Institute for Sensing and Embedded Network Systems Engineeringhttp://isense.fau.edu/index.php has a paid training opportunity for (10) CE/CS students. I-SENSE is developing a new training course for Telit (one of the largest providers of machine to machine (M2M) technologies) at Telit’s (ILS Technology) offices in Boca Raton.

The training is scheduled for the week of May 9-13 from 9AM-6PM. Lunch will be provided each day and students are paid a stipend of $600 after completion of the full five day/40 hour training course.

To qualify for these positions, students must be a sophomore or above and have passed the following course with a “B” or better:

  • COP2220; Introduction to Programming in C (Five positions)
  • Either CDA3331C; Introduction to Microprocessor System or CDA4630; Introduction to Embedded System (Five positions)
